Agile Methodology

Agile is an iterative approach to project management and software development that emphasizes collaboration, customer feedback, and small, rapid releases. Teams work in sprints to deliver portions of the project incrementally.

Key Projects:

  • Digital Banking Platform Enhancement
  • Crew Pairing and Rostering Integration
  • Data Migration
...

In Agile, requirements and solutions evolve through collaboration between cross-functional teams. There is a strong focus on continuous improvement, and teams review progress regularly to adapt and adjust the plan.

Waterfall Methodology

Waterfall is a linear, sequential project management methodology that moves through phases such as requirement gathering, design, development, testing, and deployment in a step-by-step manner.

Key Projects:

  • Payments Gateway Implementation for E-commerce
  • Cargo Systems
...

Waterfall is suitable for projects where requirements are well-defined from the beginning and are unlikely to change. Each phase must be completed before the next phase begins, making it a more rigid and structured approach.
